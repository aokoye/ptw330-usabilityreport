Usability Research

Methodology

Team-member facilitators conducted usability testing across two note-taking apps: Notion and Obsidian. Team members observed and interviewed one (or two) users for a total of five participants. Each participant was asked to complete a series of tasks in either Notion or Obsidian.

Participants completed a questionnaire before the interview. Facilitators reviewed the responses for exclusion criteria. Consent forms were filled out by the participant collected. Facilitators used a script to standardize the team’s data collection method. Then we conducted remote, qualitative usability tests using the think-aloud protocol. Researcher notes and user survey results were compiled and compared. Noteworthy findings or discrepancies between field testing and user reflection are reported in the Usability Results section.

Participants

In alignment with Jakob Nielsen’s findings that testing 5 users reveals 85% of problems, the team will recruit six people to balance the number of users per app. Team members recruited one or two users through their personal network.

Recruitment Criteria

Priorities:

  • Participants who do not have experience with either Notion or Obsidian.
  • Participants may have experience with other note-taking apps.

Exclusions:

  • Participants who have previously downloaded Notion or Obsidian.

Consent

Participants signed a consent form. Their consent gave the facilitator permission to record the user’s audio and screen activity for data collection purposes.

Procedure

  • Choose one app for the user to test: Obsidian or Notion.
  • Ask participant to complete the Pre-interview Questionnaire.
  • Review Pre-Interview data for exclusion criteria.
  • Ask participant to sign consent.
  • Welcome the user.
  • Ask opening questions.
  • Introduce the “Think Aloud Protocol.”
  • Set up recording.
  • Ask user to complete tasks:
    • Download the app.
    • Create a New Note.
    • Format a note.
    • Assign a note to a category or move to a folder (Notion) or “vault” (Obsidian).
    • Access a note.
    • Use a template to create a note.
  • Time Managements of User Tasks
    • Allow the user to proceed for up to three minutes - until you don’t feel like it’s producing any value or the user becomes highly frustrated.
    • Proceed with tasks until complete or time runs out.
    • Stop Tasks and move on to Closing Questions after 40 minutes.
  • Ask follow-up Closing Questions.
  • Thank the User.

About the Session

Participants joined a 60-minute recorded session in person or on Zoom via their personal device. The session proceeded in the following format:

  • 5 minutes: Introduction
  • 5 minutes: Initial questions
  • 5 minutes: Set up screen-sharing
  • 40 minutes: Tasks administered; researcher asks follow-up questions after each task
  • 5 minutes: Final survey

Participants chose the device to use for task completion. They were prompted to explain why they selected that device.

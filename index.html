Introduction to Usability Report for Note-Taking Applications (Apps)

Note taking is a critical, organizational skill applied across our personal and professional lives. Note taking is a relevant skill to everyday tasks. A variety of note-taking applications are available to support users’ needs for professional, educational, and personal use. There are options to choose from when selecting a note-taking app. The features and actions within such apps ensure the options meet the user’s needs. It is also important to research usability with hopes of improving and/or developing a better product.

This usability report outlines the results of a study designed to evaluate a new user’s experience with two note-taking apps: Obsidian and Notion. This report, developed by Team E.S.C. (Everyone Stay Calm), analyzes the successes, usefulness, and pain points of a new user’s experience when onboarding the two apps. Based on the findings of the study, the report outlines a set of recommendations for a note-taking app prototype. The results reveal considerations that will serve our product manager persona. Research results also inform how someone might align UX with Calm Tech principles.

Notion and Obsidian apps are two current, popular resources on the market for note taking. Both apps are applicable to this usability analysis and report. Each app offers worthwhile features dependent upon a user’s needs. This study will take an objective point of view that details what works and does not work for the user of each app.

The following report shares the testing of six user experiences with the note-taking apps. The observational study looks at how users access each app, followed by four key tasks pertinent to note taking. A close look is taken at how a first-time user applies instructions and responds to actions within the app. The report begins with background information about the Notion and Obsidian apps. It includes an in-depth look at the workflows of each app, the methodology for user testing, and the elements of UX design. An analysis of the methodology for testing and data provides insight into effectiveness of the note-taking apps. Finally, a recommendation for an app prototype completes the process.

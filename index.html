Introduction to Usability Report for Note-Taking Applications (Apps)

Note taking is a critical, organizational skill applied across our personal and professional lives. There are a variety of note-taking apps with features and actions to ensure the app meets a user’s needs.

Team ESC (Everyone Stay Calm) developed a usability test to evaluate a new user’s experience with two note-taking apps: Obsidian and Notion. This report analyzes the successes, usefulness, and pain points of a new user’s experience during the onboarding process of each app. The report outlines a set of recommendations for a note-taking app prototype. The findings of the usability test and Calm Technology principles for non-intrusive design are integrated within the recommendations. The results reveal considerations that will serve our product manager persona in the development of a competitive product.

Notion and Obsidian are two current, popular note-taking apps. This study details what works and does not work for the user of each app.

The following report shares the results of five participants who tested the note-taking apps. The observational study looks at how users access each app, followed by six key tasks pertinent to onboarding and basic note taking. A close look is taken at how a first-time user applies instructions and responds to the app interface of Notion or Obsidian.
